About Me

The Artist

Adi Oren

Hi, I'm Adi Oren, a professional makeup artist currently based in Singapore. Originally from Israel, I spent several years working in the tech industry before relocating with my family — first to Bangkok, and now to Singapore.

Moving to Bangkok was a turning point in my life. It challenged everything I knew and pushed me far beyond my comfort zone into a completely new culture, language, and way of living. Instead of holding back, I chose to explore. I tried new things, met people from all over the world and unexpectedly discovered the world of makeup.

What started as curiosity quickly became a true passion. I trained at an international makeup academy in Bangkok, studying intensively for a full year under top educators from Europe and the United States, alongside talented students from around the world.

My Training Included

  • Bridal makeup — Western, Asian, Indian, Thai
  • Fashion & editorial makeup
  • TV & stage makeup
  • Special effects
  • Day & evening makeup
  • Skin tone and skin type analysis
  • Makeup history and advanced techniques

After graduating, I began working and teaching — turning my passion into a profession.

My Philosophy

My journey taught me three key things:

  • Don't be afraid of change — it opens unexpected doors.
  • Creativity can be learned — anyone can develop it.
  • Keep trying — growth comes from action.

Your most radiant day

Your wedding day is one of the most meaningful and unforgettable moments of your life — and your makeup should reflect that.

Bridal makeup is not just about looking beautiful; it is about creating a timeless, flawless look that enhances your natural features while lasting perfectly from the first look to the last dance.

My approach focuses on refined, elegant beauty — soft, radiant skin, delicate sculpting, and harmonious neutral tones that elevate your natural glow without overpowering it. This aesthetic remains the most desired among modern brides for its sophistication and longevity.

A Tailored, Personal Approach

Every bride is unique — and so is every makeup design. I carefully customise each look based on:

  • Skin type and texture
  • Facial structure and proportions
  • Personal style and preferences
  • Lighting conditions (daylight / indoor / evening)
  • Photography requirements

The goal is to highlight your strongest features while subtly softening any areas you prefer to refine — creating a balanced, photogenic, and long-lasting result.

Longevity & Professional Techniques

Bridal makeup requires advanced techniques and high-performance products to ensure:

  • Long-lasting wear (12–16 hours)
  • Resistance to humidity and heat (especially relevant in Singapore)
  • Flawless appearance in both real life and photography
  • Minimal need for touch-ups

I use only premium, professional-grade products selected specifically for bridal performance.

Bridal Trial — A Key Step

A trial session is highly recommended prior to your wedding day. This allows us to:

  • Define your ideal look together
  • Test tones, textures, and intensity
  • Ensure full comfort and confidence
  • Finalise every detail in advance

By the time of your wedding, everything is perfectly planned — leaving you relaxed and confident.

Choosing the Right Makeup Artist

Selecting the right bridal makeup artist is essential. When making your decision, consider:

  • Proven Experience & Recommendations — Work with an artist trusted by real brides and clients.
  • Portfolio Quality — Review previous bridal work to assess style, consistency, and attention to detail.
  • Professional Products — Ensure only high-quality, long-wear products are used.
  • Logistics & Availability — Choose an artist who can accommodate your schedule and location seamlessly.

The Experience
My role is not only to create your look but to provide a calm, professional, and luxurious experience on your wedding day. From our first consultation to the final touch, every detail is handled with precision, care, and a deep understanding of bridal beauty.

What is SFX Makeup?

Have you ever wondered who creates the terrifying creatures, aging effects, and otherworldly characters you see on screen?

Special Effects (SFX) Makeup is the art of transforming a person's appearance beyond natural beauty into something entirely new, often dramatic, and sometimes unrecognizable.

While traditional makeup focuses on enhancing features and refining beauty, SFX makeup is designed to create characters — from subtle aging and realistic injuries to complex transformations such as witches, aliens, monsters, and supernatural beings.

The Role of SFX in Film & Production

In film, television, and stage productions, visual realism is essential. SFX makeup plays a key role in building believable characters and supporting storytelling.

From bruises, cuts, burns, and scars to full character reconstruction, every effect must appear authentic under high-definition cameras and professional lighting conditions.

Whether it's a horror scene, a science-fiction character, or an age transformation spanning decades — SFX makeup is what brings these visions to life.

Technical Expertise & Materials

Creating realistic special effects requires both artistic vision and technical mastery. My work is based on professional training in stage and film makeup, including specialized SFX techniques. I have hands-on experience working with a wide range of materials such as:

  • Latex and silicone for prosthetics
  • Gel-based textures and skin simulation
  • Artificial blood and wound effects
  • Rubber-based materials for structure and flexibility

These materials allow me to build layered, highly realistic effects tailored to each production.

Process & Attention to Detail

SFX makeup is a multi-stage process that requires time, precision, and patience. Each transformation is carefully constructed — from sculpting and application to colouring and blending — often requiring extended preparation time to achieve a seamless, believable result.

Attention to detail is critical. Every texture, shade, and contour is designed to ensure the final look appears completely natural on camera.

Why It Matters

Special Effects Makeup is essential in any production where characters undergo physical transformation. From aging a character by decades to creating cinematic creatures, SFX makeup enables directors and creators to translate imagination into reality.

It is a fundamental part of modern storytelling across:

  • Film & Television
  • Theatre productions
  • Commercials & advertising
  • Creative and editorial projects

From zombies and fantasy characters to realistic trauma effects — SFX makeup creates the visual language that makes stories believable.
